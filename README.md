## I think good data work starts with understanding the business and the people in it.

There's rarely a single "right" architecture, but there's almost always a good path forward once you understand what a team actually needs from their data. That approach shapes everything from how I model a warehouse to how I run a code review.

---
### At work 
I'm a Lead Analytics Engineer at Lumen Technologies, where I've spent the last two and a half years building out a marketing data platform inside a Fortune 500 org. That's meant designing a medallion warehouse in Microsoft Fabric from scratch, cutting over a thousand fields of raw Adobe Analytics data down to a semantic model that a 300-person marketing team actually trusts, and helping a group of analysts grow into engineers. The technical problems are interesting. The organizational ones are harder and more rewarding.

### Outside of work 
I'm building a cross-stitch pattern generator as a SaaS product -- Python/FastAPI backend, React frontend, Delta-E color matching against DMC thread palettes. It started as a project for my wife and turned into a genuinely interesting product and customer discovery problem. Building something end-to-end for real users teaches you things that enterprise work rarely does.

### My current stack 
dbt, Snowflake, Microsoft Fabric, Python, SQL, PySpark. I pick up new technologies quickly and apply best practices to them fluently within 1-2 months.

If you want a sense of how I think about data modeling and architecture, the marketing-analytics-foundation repo is a good starting point.

---
### What I'm looking for 
I love working in roles where data work connects to outcomes that matter both to internal stakeholders and the people who use our products. I want to solve real problems by informing decisions that help build something that lasts. I'm drawn to early-stage companies, healthtech, fintech, and B2B SaaS, and I'm open to other conversations as well. If something I've built or written resonates with what you're working on, I'd love to talk.
